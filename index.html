Map-to-Image Change Detection

Mapping land cover is really important to monitor and protect the Earth, but analysing image data to map land cover takes a lot of time and effort. This work was a collaboration I spearheaded between Aberystwyth University Earth Observation and Ecosystem Dynamics Research Group, Welsh Government and Geosmart Decisions to develop a new method to simplify land cover mapping by updating old mapped land cover regions using new image data, map-to-image change detection.

I coded and implemented several variations of this method on nearly 200 satellite images of Wales from between 1990 and 2017 to map changes in Coniferous forest extent. I performed further data analysis on the outputs to measure how, when, where and when forest extent was changing and delivered a report currently being used to inform government policy.

Modelling an Ecological Niche

Global biodiversity loss is occurring faster than ever and is an enormous problem. Statistical models have become very important in identifying potential habitats and preferred ecological conditions. In this study I constructed three models of Red Kite habitat in Wales to compare and understand the differences and inform conservation practices. I sourced and processed freely available ecological and red kite sighting data in R and ESRI ArcMap to create maximum entropy, boosted regression trees and random forest models.

Twitter Engagment Analysis

Twitter is an free and growing data source that we can anlyse to find information about what people are doing, thinking and buying. For this personal project I downloaded and developed some basic analysis in python of what I’d been doing on my twitter account - @GradyMaddie. I devised code to clean, sort and extract what times I tweet (too early in the morning), the engagement levels on tweets (higher later in the day when people more people are awake) and what subjects they were on. I’ve tweeted 142 times. People have engaged with 14.79% of my tweets and 64.08% of my tweets are retweets of another users content.
